Me · Myself · I

Three voices under one God

Me

I wake with dust on my name,
breath borrowed,
waiting to be told who I am.

The mirror speaks softly,
but the soul listens harder.

Myself

Between flesh and prayer
I hesitate,
neither fallen nor risen.

Lord, rearrange me
without breaking me.

I

I am not the author.
I am the sentence
still being written.

If I stand, it is because
grace remembered my name.

“For in Him we live, and move, and have our being.” — Acts 17:28
